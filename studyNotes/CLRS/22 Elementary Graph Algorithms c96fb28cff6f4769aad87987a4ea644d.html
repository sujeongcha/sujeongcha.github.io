B.4 Graphs

  • directed graph (digraph): E is a binary relation on V, self-loop allowed undirected graph: no direction, self-loop NOT allowed
  • adjacent: v is adjacent to u if (u,v) is an edge degree: in-degree + out-degree (0 degree → "isolated")
  • simple path: if all vertices in the path are distinct simple cycle: cycle with distinct vertices simple directed graph: directed graph with no self-loop
  • connected: for undirected; every vertex is reachable from all other vertices strongly connected: for directed; every two vertices are reachable from each other
  • complete graph: undirected graph in which every pair of vertices is adjacent bipartite graph: undirected graph in which V can be partitioned into two sets
  • forest: acyclic, undirected graph (free) tree: connected, acyclic, undirected graph DAG: acyclic, directed graph
  • multigraph: undirected graph with multiple edges between vertices and self-loops hypergraph: undirected graph with hyperedges connecting an arbitrary subset of vertices
  • contraction: in an undirected graph, u, v are contracted into a single vertex

22.2 Breadth-First-Search

  • explores the edges of G to “discover” every vertex that is reachable from s (source vertex)
  • discovers all vertices at distance k from s before discovering any vertices at distance k + 1
  • the result may depend upon the order in which the neighbors of a given vertex are visited in line 12: the breadth-first tree may vary, but the distances d computed by the algorithm will not

22.3 Depth-First-Search

  • Unlike breadth-first search, whose predecessor subgraph forms a tree, the predecessor subgraph produced by a depth-first search may be composed of several trees (depth-first-forest)
  • depth-first search also timestamps each vertex (v.d and v.f)
  • running time: Θ(V+E)\Theta(V+E)Θ(V+E)﻿

Classification of edges (u, v)

  1. Tree edges (v WHITE): edges in the depth-first forest
  1. Back edges (v GRAY): connecting u to an ancestor in a depth-first tree, including self-loops
  1. Forward edges (v BLACK): nontree edges connecting u to a descendant in a depth-first tree
  1. Cross edges (v BLACK): all other edges (between vertices in the same depth-first tree, as long as one vertex is not an ancestor of the other, or between vertices in different depth-first trees)
  • In a DFS of an undirected graph G, every edge of G is either a tree edge or a back edge
